									<p id='intelliTxt' class="intro" data-module="article-intro">Changing the bedsheets is an easy chore that you should take on every two to four weeks (or less if the sheets become dirty sooner than that). There are two parts for bedsheets: the top sheet and the bottom sheet. Both of these sheets should be changed at the same time. Don't leave one sheet on and change the other.

									<h2 class="header Heading3">Removing Dirty Sheets</h2>
							<ul>
								<li class="step ">
														<div class="stepMeat">
																<div itemprop="step">
									<p>When it's time to take your dirty sheets off of the bed, start at the top. Make sure you have a laundry basket handy. The first sheet should easily come off the bed with a good yank. Throw it into the laundry basket. The next sheet will be tucked underneath the mattress, so you'll have to peel this sheet off at its corners. Once you've untucked the lower sheet, throw it into the laundry basket. You should be left with the mattress cover.</p>								</div>
							</div>
						</li>
															</ul>
									<h2 class="header Heading3">New Sheets</h2>
							<ul>
								<li class="step ">
														<div class="stepMeat">
																<div itemprop="step">
									<p>To put new sheets onto the bed, first separate your two sheets. Take the sheet with the elastic at its corners first. Find one of the corners of the sheet, and tuck it a few inches under a corner of your mattress. Now stretch the sheet to the opposite corner of the bed. With the first corner still in place, tuck the sheet's opposite corner into the opposite corner of the mattress. Now tuck the remaining corners in, and your first sheet is on.</p>

<p>Take the other clean sheet and spread it evenly across the bed. Fold about a foot of the sheet down from the head of the bed (this will make room for your pillows). Tuck the edges of the sheet under the mattress.</p>

<p>If you want a blanket on your bed, now is the time to spread it on top of the other sheets. Tuck the bottom of the blanket under the mattress and make hospital corners (see the eHow resource link below for hospital corners). You should now have a newly made bed with fresh sheets, ready for a good night's sleep. Don't forget the pillowcases.</p>								</div>
							</div>
						</li>

	</div>

															</ul>
									<h2 class="header Heading3">Washing Dirty Sheets</h2>
							<ul>
								<li class="step ">
														<div class="stepMeat">
																<div itemprop="step">
									<p>Take the dirty sheets that you've put into the laundry basket and bring them to your washing machine (or if the tag on your sheets says to dry clean them, then simply do that and ignore the rest of this step). Don't wash your sheets with any other clothes. Set the washing machine for cold water, and use the most gentle cycle on your washer. Avoid bleach, and use a regular laundry detergent. Don't put your sheets into the washer until the detergent has dissolved in the water.</p>

<p>When your sheets are done washing, it is always best to hang them on a line to dry. But if you must use a dryer, make sure to use the lowest heat setting on the dryer.<br />
When your sheets are dry, fold them up and store them away for the next time you have to change the dirty sheets on your bed.</p>								</div>
							</div>

 <h1 class="entry-title">20 Things to Do with Old Bed Sheets</h1>
 <div class="entry-byline">
  <span id="time-diff"></span> by <a rel="author" href="http://archive.blisstree.com/author/deborahn/" title="Posts by Deborah Ng" rel="author">Deborah Ng</a> | 
  <a href="http://archive.blisstree.com/live/20-things-to-do-with-old-bed-sheets/#comments">15 Comments</a> | 
  <a href="#top" pid="25851" class="view-count view-count-25851" onclick="show_modal_window('share-tip-window');">Share a Tip</a>
  <span id="edit-post-link"></span>
  
 </div>

 
 <div class="entry-content">
  <div class="entry-post"> 
   <p class="MsoNormal">If you’re like me, you probably have several old bed sheets that are a bit worn, or they don’t match any of your bedroom décor. You don’t want to throw your sheets out, but they’re not really doing much of anything either. What’s a frugalist to do?</p>
<p class="MsoNormal">Behold: 20 Things to Do With Old Bedsheets</p>
<ol>
<li>Use them for drop clothes for painting projects</li>
<li>Make a laundry bag</li>
<li>Use it to make gift wrap</li>
<li>Cut them into thin strips and use them to knit or crochet</li>
<li>Cut them up and use them to make and stuff throw pillows</li>
<li>Take them to the beach and use for picnics</li>
<li>Crochet them into rag rugs</li>
<li>Turn them into linen napkins and table cloths</li>
<li>Cut them up and use them for dusting</li>
<li>Use them for quilt backing</li>
<li>Donate them to homeless shelters</li>
<li>Cover fruit trees to protect from frost</li>
<li>Tear into strips and use to tie your tomato plants to stakes</li>
<li>Cover old furniture and other items collecting dust in attics, garages and basements</li>
<li>Make drawstring gift bags</li>
<li>Make kitchen curtains</li>
<li>Let children drape over chairs to make tents and forts</li>
<li>Use for picnic or camping table cloth</li>
<li>Use to slip cover chairs</li>
<li>Freecycle</li>
</ol>
<p class="MsoNormal"><o :p> </o></p>

									<p id='intelliTxt' class="intro" data-module="article-intro">If you notice an unpleasant odor emanating from your bed sheets, your sheets probably have trapped bacteria inside the fabric fibers that are creating the distasteful smells. Instead of living with the odors or tossing the sheets, wash them thoroughly to remove the foul bacteria. Although the cleaning process may involve several steps, you can successfully get smells out of bed sheets and restore your bedding to its former clean, sweet-smelling condition.

			<h2 class="Heading2">Instructions</h2>
	
    <section>

		<ol id="intelliTxt" class="steps">
						<li class="section">
								<ul>
								<li class="step ">
															<span class="stepNumber">1</span>
														<div class="stepMeat">
																<div itemprop="step">
									<p>Check the fabric content and washing instructions for your sheets to determine the best way to machine-wash them before you proceed with the odor-removing process.</p>								</div>
							</div>
						</li>
																<li class="step ">
															<span class="stepNumber">2</span>
														<div class="stepMeat">
																<div itemprop="step">
									<p>Place the sheets into the washing machine. Add either chlorine bleach or color-safe bleach to the bleach dispenser in the washing machine (chlorine bleach for white sheets and color-safe bleach for colored sheets). Consult the package label for the recommended amount of bleach. Add heavy-duty laundry detergent to the washing machine also. Set the water temperature to the warmest temperature recommended by the manufacturer and wash the sheets in a regular wash cycle. If you do not have washing instructions, wash the sheets in warm water.</p>								</div>
							</div>
						</li>
												<li class="googleAd">
								<div id="DMINSTR" type="adimpression" data-type="adTracking" name="&amp;adunit_id=ca-ehow_300x250&amp;ad_unit_type=CNT&amp;ad_unit_network=GAP&amp;revenue_basis=CPC&amp;ad_position=-&amp;revenue_tag=home_housekeeping">
		<div id="GoogleAdsense300x250"></div>
		
	</div>
						</li>
																<li class="step ">
															<span class="stepNumber">3</span>
														<div class="stepMeat">
																<div itemprop="step">
									<p>Remove the sheets from the washing machine when the cycle ends. Shake them out to remove wrinkles as you place them into the laundry basket.</p>								</div>
							</div>
						</li>
																<li class="step ">
															<span class="stepNumber">4</span>
														<div class="stepMeat">
																<div itemprop="step">
									<p>Hang the sheets outside on a clothesline. If you do not have a clothesline, use a dryer instead; however, drying the sheets outside in the fresh air and sunshine can be effective for removing fabric odors. Allow the sheets to hang until they dry completely.</p>								</div>
							</div>
						</li>
																<li class="step ">
															<span class="stepNumber">5</span>
														<div class="stepMeat">
																<div itemprop="step">
									<p>Take the dry sheets down from the clothesline (or from the dryer) and assess their smell. If you still smell odors in the fabric, proceed with the next step.</p>								</div>
							</div>
						</li>
																<li class="step ">
															<span class="stepNumber">6</span>
														<div class="stepMeat">
																<div itemprop="step">
									<p>Spread the sheets out flat on the floor. Sprinkle baking soda liberally over the entire surface area of both the top sheet and the fitted sheet. Roll the sheets up to hold the baking soda in place and place the rolled sheets into the large bin. Cover the bin and allow the sheets to sit in the enclosed bin for two to three hours.</p>								</div>
							</div>
						</li>
																<li class="step ">
															<span class="stepNumber">7</span>
														<div class="stepMeat">
																<div itemprop="step">
									<p>Remove the sheets from the bin. Shake off the excess baking soda and repeat the washing and drying steps again to finish removing the odors from the sheets.</p>								</div>
							</div>
						</li>
															</ul>
						</li>
					</ol>
